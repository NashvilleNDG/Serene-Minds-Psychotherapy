What Is Therapy for Children and Teenagers?

Child and teen therapy — also called child counseling or adolescent therapy — is a specialized form of psychotherapy tailored to the developmental, emotional, and behavioral needs of young people. Unlike adult therapy, which relies primarily on verbal communication, child and adolescent therapy uses age-appropriate techniques — play, art, structured activities, and developmentally adapted conversation — to help young people express and process what they are experiencing.

Therapy gives children and teenagers a safe, confidential space to explore their thoughts and feelings — separate from the pressures of school, family, and social life. It helps them develop emotional regulation skills, build resilience, understand their own patterns of thinking and behavior, and navigate the challenges of growing up with greater confidence and self-awareness.

For parents, therapy also provides guidance on how to best support your child at home — because the most effective child therapy is always a partnership between the therapist, the child, and the family. At Serene Minds Psychotherapy, all sessions are conducted via secure, HIPAA-compliant online video — making consistent, high-quality child and teen therapy accessible to families anywhere in Florida.

What We Help Children and Teens With

Fram works with children and teenagers navigating a wide range of emotional, behavioral, and developmental challenges — always with an approach tailored to the young person's age, personality, and specific needs.

😰 Anxiety in Children and Teens

Anxiety is the most common mental health challenge among young people — affecting approximately 1 in 3 teenagers before adulthood. Whether it shows up as school refusal, separation anxiety, social anxiety, excessive worry, or physical symptoms like stomachaches and headaches, therapy helps children and teens understand their anxiety and develop practical tools to manage it confidently.

😔 Depression and Low Mood

Depression in young people often looks different from adult depression — irritability, social withdrawal, declining school performance, and loss of interest in activities they once loved are common signs, and early intervention makes a significant difference. Therapy helps depressed children and teens understand their emotions, challenge negative thinking patterns, and rebuild their sense of hope and motivation.

🧠 ADHD and Attention Difficulties

Children and teenagers with ADHD face unique challenges — not just with attention and focus, but with self-regulation, impulse control, organization, and self-esteem. Therapy helps young people with ADHD understand how their brain works, develop practical strategies for managing daily challenges, and build confidence in their genuine strengths.

😢 Trauma and Abuse

Children who have experienced trauma — abuse, neglect, accidents, loss, or witnessing violence — need specialized, trauma-informed support. Fram works with children and teens using gentle, age-appropriate, evidence-based approaches that prioritize safety, stability, and gradual healing at the young person's own pace.

😤 Behavioral Challenges

Persistent behavioral difficulties — defiance, aggression, lying, rule-breaking, or conduct problems — are often a child's way of communicating an unmet need or unprocessed emotion. Therapy helps identify what is driving the behavior, develops more effective responses from parents and caregivers, and gives the child alternative ways to express and manage their needs.

🏫 School-Related Struggles

Academic underperformance, school refusal, bullying, social difficulties, test anxiety, and learning-related stress are among the most common reasons parents seek therapy for their children. Fram works with families and, when appropriate, coordinates with schools to provide wraparound support that helps young people thrive in the classroom.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Transitions

Divorce, separation, relocation, a new sibling, a parent's illness, or any significant family change can profoundly affect a child's sense of security and wellbeing. Therapy provides children and teens with a safe, stable space to process these transitions and develop the resilience to adapt to change without losing their sense of self.

🌍 Cultural Identity and Belonging

For children and teenagers from South Asian, immigrant, or multicultural backgrounds — navigating between the values of home and the expectations of peers, school, and broader society — the struggle for identity and belonging can be intense and isolating. Fram offers culturally sensitive therapy in English, Gujarati, and Hindi, providing a rare space where young people feel genuinely understood.

😴 Sleep Problems and Physical Symptoms

Anxiety, depression, trauma, and stress frequently manifest in children as physical symptoms — stomachaches, headaches, sleep disturbances, and fatigue without a clear medical cause. Therapy addresses the emotional roots of these physical presentations, helping children develop mind-body awareness and healthier sleep and coping habits.

How Fram Works With Children and Teens: Evidence-Based Approaches

CBT adapted for children and teenagers is one of the most extensively researched and effective therapeutic approaches for young people — particularly for anxiety, depression, and behavioral difficulties. Child-adapted CBT uses age-appropriate language, visual tools, and structured activities to help young people identify the connections between their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. Teenagers engage with CBT in a more conversational, collaborative way — learning to recognize and challenge the distorted thinking patterns that fuel their distress. Over time CBT gives young people a practical, portable toolkit for managing difficult emotions and situations independently.

Play is the natural language of childhood. For younger children who do not yet have the verbal capacity to express complex emotions, play therapy provides a powerful alternative medium for communication and healing. Through carefully structured play activities — drawing, storytelling, sand play, puppets, and creative expression — Fram helps children process difficult experiences, express emotions safely, and develop new ways of relating to themselves and others. Play therapy is particularly effective for children who have experienced trauma, loss, or significant behavioral difficulties.

DBT adapted for adolescents is especially effective for teenagers who experience intense, rapidly shifting emotions — including teenagers dealing with depression, self-harm urges, eating difficulties, or explosive anger. DBT-A teaches four core skill sets — mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness — in language and formats specifically designed for adolescent brains and lives. The skills DBT teaches are practical, immediately applicable, and produce visible improvements in daily functioning relatively quickly.

Children and teenagers who have experienced trauma need an approach that prioritizes safety and trust above all else. Fram's trauma-informed approach ensures that every session creates a predictable, safe environment where young people can process their experiences at their own pace — never feeling pushed or overwhelmed. Trauma-informed therapy integrates elements of narrative therapy, somatic awareness, and gradual exposure to help children and teens reclaim a sense of safety and agency in their own lives.

Teenagers especially can feel reluctant, resistant, or compelled rather than invited to attend therapy. Motivational Interviewing addresses this directly — meeting each young person exactly where they are, exploring their own values and goals, and building genuine internal motivation for change rather than compliance with adult expectations. Fram is a certified MI practitioner and is experienced in engaging even highly resistant adolescents in meaningful therapeutic work.

The most effective child and adolescent therapy always includes the parents. Depending on the child's age and presenting concerns, Fram involves parents through regular check-ins, parent guidance sessions, and collaborative goal-setting. Parents receive specific, practical guidance on how to support their child's therapeutic work at home — because the most powerful changes in a child's life happen in the family environment, not just in the therapy room.

Signs Your Child or Teen May Benefit from Therapy

  • ✓ Persistent sadness, irritability, or emotional withdrawal lasting more than two weeks
  • ✓ Excessive worry, fear, or anxiety that interferes with daily life
  • ✓ Refusing to go to school or significant drop in academic performance
  • ✓ Significant behavioral changes — aggression, defiance, or rule-breaking
  • ✓ Withdrawal from friends, family, or activities they used to enjoy
  • ✓ Difficulty concentrating, sitting still, or following through on tasks
  • ✓ Sleep disturbances — nightmares, difficulty falling asleep, or sleeping too much
  • ✓ Physical complaints — stomachaches, headaches — without a medical cause
  • ✓ Exposure to trauma, abuse, neglect, or witnessing violence
  • ✓ Significant family changes — divorce, relocation, bereavement, new sibling
  • ✓ Social difficulties — struggling to make or keep friends, bullying
  • ✓ Expressing hopelessness, worthlessness, or thoughts of self-harm
Important: If your child or teenager is expressing thoughts of suicide or self-harm, please call or text 988 immediately or go to your nearest emergency room. This page is not monitored 24/7 and is not for emergencies.
