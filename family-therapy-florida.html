What Is Family Therapy?

Family therapy — also called family counseling — is psychotherapy that works with the family as a whole rather than only one individual. It creates a structured, neutral space where every member can be heard and valued.

Unlike individual therapy, family therapy examines the patterns, dynamics, and communication cycles that shape how your family functions. Fram helps everyone understand how each person’s behavior affects the others — and offers evidence-based tools to communicate better, resolve conflict, and rebuild connection.

Research shows family therapy can meaningfully improve family functioning, communication, and wellbeing. At Serene Minds, all sessions are secure HIPAA-compliant online video for families across Florida.

“Family therapy does not take sides. It creates a space where every voice matters — and where real change becomes possible for everyone.”

How Fram Approaches Family Therapy: Evidence-Based Methods

Structural family therapy focuses on organization — boundaries between members, family hierarchy, and subsystems. When structure is unclear — reversed roles, rigid or diffuse boundaries, scapegoating, triangulation — problems follow. This approach maps patterns and uses direct interventions to support healthy functioning.

CBT for families addresses negative thought patterns and distorted beliefs members bring to interactions — and how those patterns fuel recurring conflict. Members learn to examine automatic interpretations and develop more accurate, generous understandings of each other.

Every family has stories about itself. When narratives center only on conflict or failure, change feels impossible. Narrative therapy separates problems from people and co-creates empowering stories that open new possibilities.

This approach highlights strengths and exceptions: when does your family get along? What does “at our best” look like? What small shift would help this week? It is forward-looking and reminds everyone that change is possible.

Not every member arrives equally willing to engage — common with parents or teens. MI meets each person where they are, explores personal values and reasons for change, and builds internal motivation instead of compliance. Fram is a certified MI practitioner with decades of experience.

Structured learning about mental health conditions, developmental stages, trauma’s impact on systems, and more helps families support one another more effectively. Understanding reduces blame and increases teamwork.

Signs Your Family May Benefit from Family Therapy

  • ✓ The same arguments happen repeatedly without resolution
  • ✓ Family members have stopped talking to each other openly
  • ✓ A parent and child are in persistent, escalating conflict
  • ✓ A family member is struggling with mental health and the family does not know how to help
  • ✓ The family is going through a major transition — divorce, relocation, new baby, loss
  • ✓ Siblings are in ongoing, damaging conflict
  • ✓ You are a blended family navigating complex dynamics
  • ✓ A family member has experienced trauma that is affecting the whole family
  • ✓ Family members feel emotionally disconnected from each other
  • ✓ Cultural or generational differences are creating ongoing tension
  • ✓ A parent feels they have lost connection with their teenager
  • ✓ The family has experienced a bereavement and is struggling to support each other
