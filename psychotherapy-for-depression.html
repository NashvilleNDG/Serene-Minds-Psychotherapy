What Is Psychotherapy for Depression?

Psychotherapy for depression—sometimes called therapy for depression or counseling—is a structured, evidence-based treatment where you work one-on-one with a licensed therapist to understand and overcome depression.

Unlike medication alone, psychotherapy gets to the root drivers of low mood and teaches practical tools for emotional regulation, coping, and behavior change. Some clients want treatment for depression without drugs, while others combine therapy with medical care.

The psychotherapy of depression helps identify negative thought loops, unresolved pain, and life stress patterns that keep you stuck. It supports mild, moderate, and severe depression when matched to the right plan.

“Psychotherapy for depression helps address root causes, not just symptoms.”

Types of Depression We Treat

Depression shows up differently for different people. Fram supports clients across these common presentations:

🌧️ Major Depressive Disorder

Persistent sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest that impacts daily functioning. Therapy interrupts negative cycles and rebuilds motivation.

🌤️ Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)

Long-term, lower-grade depression that can feel “normal.” Therapy helps identify patterns and restore energy and purpose.

👶 Postpartum Depression

Overwhelming sadness, anxiety, or disconnection after childbirth. Fram provides non-judgmental support for healing and adjustment.

🍂 Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

Depressive symptoms that worsen in darker seasons. Therapy builds preventive routines and coping strategies year-round.

⚖️ Bipolar Depression

Debilitating depressive episodes within bipolar disorder. Fram helps with mood regulation skills and emotional stabilization.

🌪️ Depression with Anxiety

Sadness and chronic worry often coexist. Treatment addresses both conditions together for better balance and calm.

🌸 Depression in Women

Hormonal shifts, caregiving burden, and major life transitions can intensify symptoms. Care is tailored and gender-sensitive.

🎓 Depression in Teens and Young Adults

Young people face unique academic, social, and identity stress. Fram provides age-appropriate and compassionate support.

How Fram Treats Depression: Evidence-Based Therapy Approaches

CBT is one of the most researched therapies for depression. It helps you challenge distorted thoughts and replace them with balanced thinking and actionable behaviors.

DBT teaches practical skills in mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness for clients with high emotional intensity.

When depression drains motivation, MI helps clarify your values and strengthen internal commitment, one realistic step at a time.

Mindfulness supports a calmer relationship with thoughts and emotions, reducing the repetitive mental loops that often maintain depression.

This approach emphasizes what is working, what is possible, and concrete next steps so progress feels practical and achievable.

REBT helps identify rigid beliefs that fuel hopelessness and replaces them with healthier, realistic frameworks for daily life.

Signs That Psychotherapy for Depression May Help You

  • ✓ Persistently sad, empty, or hopeless for two weeks or more
  • ✓ Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
  • ✓ Constant fatigue or low energy
  • ✓ Sleeping too much or unable to sleep
  • ✓ Appetite changes and weight shifts
  • ✓ Difficulty focusing, remembering, or deciding
  • ✓ Worthlessness, shame, or excessive guilt
  • ✓ Withdrawing from friends and family
  • ✓ Irritability, restlessness, or anger spikes
  • ✓ Daily tasks feel overwhelming
  • ✓ Thoughts of death or self-harm
Important: If you are experiencing thoughts of suicide or self-harm, call or text 988 immediately, or go to your nearest emergency room. This page is not monitored 24/7 and is not for emergencies.
