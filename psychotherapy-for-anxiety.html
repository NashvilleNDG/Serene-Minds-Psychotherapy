What Is Psychotherapy for Anxiety?

Psychotherapy for anxiety is structured, evidence-based treatment that helps you understand what drives your anxiety and build skills that last. Therapy targets why your nervous system stays on high alert—not only the surface symptoms.

Anxiety is the most common mental health condition in the United States, affecting tens of millions of adults each year. Many never get care, believing they are “just a worrier.” Therapy shows another path.

Fram helps you name the thoughts, triggers, and patterns behind your anxiety and practice tools grounded in research. Sessions are fully online, HIPAA-compliant, and available across Florida from home.

“Anxiety is not a character flaw. It is a learned response—and what has been learned can be unlearned.”

Types of Anxiety We Treat

Anxiety is not one-size-fits-all. Fram works with everyday stress through complex anxiety disorders.

😰 Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Persistent, excessive worry about everyday life that feels impossible to shut off. Therapy helps interrupt chronic worry and build real calm.

🫀 Panic Disorder

Sudden surges of fear with physical symptoms that can feel like an emergency. You learn to short-circuit panic cycles and return to places you have avoided.

👥 Social Anxiety

Intense fear of judgment or embarrassment—not simple shyness. Therapy challenges the beliefs underneath and restores confidence in connection.

🔄 Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Intrusive thoughts and rituals that drain energy. Fram uses specialized, evidence-based approaches for obsessions and compulsions.

😨 PTSD

Anxiety rooted in trauma keeps the body in survival mode. Therapy helps your nervous system register safety so healing can deepen.

🌑 Health Anxiety

Persistent fear of serious illness when reassurance never lasts. Therapy addresses the anxiety beneath the worry and your relationship with bodily sensations.

🌿 Separation Anxiety

Adults too can fear separation from loved ones. Fram explores attachment patterns and strengthens a steadier sense of self.

😤 Anxiety with Depression

Worry and low mood often feed each other. Treatment addresses both together for balance and stability.

How Fram Treats Anxiety: Evidence-Based Therapy Approaches

CBT helps you spot automatic negative thoughts, test them against evidence, and practice balanced alternatives—retraining threat detection so anxiety eases at the source.

DBT builds mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal skills so you can feel anxiety without being ruled by it.

MI connects you to your own reasons for wanting relief—so facing fears feels less like a threat and more like a choice. Fram is a certified MI practitioner with decades of experience.

Mindfulness teaches you to notice anxious thoughts with distance, widening the space between trigger and reaction—and over time can calm the brain’s fear circuitry.

This approach highlights strengths, preferred future, and small next steps—forward momentum that anxiety often tries to veto.

REBT targets demanding beliefs about perfection, uncertainty, and catastrophe—replacing them with flexible thinking that lowers anxiety at the root.

Signs That Psychotherapy for Anxiety May Help You

  • ✓ You worry excessively about things others do not find threatening
  • ✓ Your mind races at night and sleep is hard to find or keep
  • ✓ You have physical symptoms—tight chest, racing heart, shortness of breath—without a clear medical cause
  • ✓ You avoid places, people, or situations because of anxiety
  • ✓ You feel on edge, irritable, or braced for something to go wrong
  • ✓ Panic attacks are shrinking your daily life
  • ✓ You spend a lot of time seeking reassurance
  • ✓ Anxiety is hurting work or school performance
  • ✓ You struggle to stay present in relationships because your mind is elsewhere
  • ✓ You use alcohol, food, or other substances to calm down
  • ✓ Intrusive thoughts feel impossible to control
  • ✓ You have been anxious so long it feels like personality
