What Is Marriage Counseling?

Marriage counseling — also called couples therapy or couples counseling — helps partners resolve conflicts, improve communication, rebuild trust, and strengthen emotional connection. It creates a neutral, structured space where both partners can speak honestly and be genuinely heard.

Unlike individual therapy, marriage counseling treats the relationship itself as the client. Fram helps you understand how histories, attachment styles, communication patterns, and unspoken expectations shape the dynamic — and gives you evidence-based tools to change it.

Research consistently shows couples therapy is effective, with many couples reporting significant improvements in relationship satisfaction. At Serene Minds, all sessions are secure HIPAA-compliant online video — available to couples throughout Miami and across Florida.

“The goal of marriage counseling is not to save a relationship at all costs. It is to help both partners build something genuinely worth staying for.”

How Fram Approaches Marriage Counseling: Evidence-Based Methods

The Gottman Method identifies patterns like criticism, contempt, defensiveness, and stonewalling, and replaces them with evidence-based skills for friendship, conflict management, and shared meaning. Fram draws on Gottman principles as a strong foundation for couples work.

EFT helps couples identify the underlying needs and fears driving conflict cycles, especially pursue-withdraw dynamics and emotional disconnection. It supports partners becoming a secure base for one another.

CBT for couples targets the automatic thoughts and assumptions that fuel conflict. Partners learn to test interpretations, reduce reactivity, and adopt more accurate, generous ways of understanding each other.

When one partner feels more motivated than the other, MI meets each person where they are and builds internal motivation rather than compliance. Fram is a certified MI practitioner with decades of experience.

This approach explores when you feel most connected, what your relationship looks like at its best, and what small changes create meaningful shifts this week — practical momentum alongside deeper work.

Sessions include structured training: active listening, non-defensive responding, de-escalation, repair after conflict, and “I” statements — skills couples can use immediately for visible daily improvement.
